EROS+PRAGMA

Sexual Desire
"Under the covers, we made friction of each
other and fiction of everything else."
-Ocean Vuong

Eros, named after the Greek god of fertility, is about sexual passion. The Greek gods saw this form of love as fiery, dangerous and irrational. It involves an all-consuming lust and a lack of control that frightened the Greek.

Yet today, we see a more interesting approach to Eros. People fiend for a type of love that reliquishes control, something passionate and intense.A lot of us hope to fall madly in love, but is that real? Is it sustainable? Longlasting? Mostly, these are romantic relationships that are shorterm, especially if you're young. Is that such a bad thing, however? Isn't it nice to think that you can have genuine passion and heat in a relationship at least once in your life, even if it doesn't last forever?






Long-Standing Love
"about us, whose loving has become so natural that it has rid itself of teeth and claws,
implements for the lovers new at love, whose jitters goad them into drawing blood"
-Lisel Mueller

Pragma is best described as a mature and realistic love. This one is a bit of a modern update of ancient Greek terms, since it was popularized in the 1970s and never explicitely used by ancient Greeks. It's meant
to summarize Greek standards of love that stands the test of time. A wholesome love that takes compromise, patience and tolerance.

If eros is about falling madly in love, then pragma is about standing in love. In this day and age, I think, mostly for young people, we want to be consumed by romantic love, but fail to understand that it's something that takes effort. Maintaing relationships isn't something that many of us think about, which is okay. You don't need to have long-lasting love if you don't want to. But if you do, you need to be prepared to handle the issues that come with it with maturity.
